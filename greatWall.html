The space frame elements are used to create a wide and high wall that will hold a large mosaic on one side and a climbing wall on the other. The plates on the side of the wall that makes up the climbing wall are perforated plates that allow for the montage of foot-hand grips so that visitors can climb up the wall. To aid in the ascent, a top rope system is used, insuring both fun and safety while climbing. As the climb is not a high climb, the belayer is not really needed nor actually the use of a top rope system. The foot-hand grips are created from making casts from scans of various parts of sculpture or parts of sculptures from ancient Rome and Greece times. They are mounted to the space frame with standard industry nuts and bolts for that purpose.
