Space Art Tunnel


for digital born artworks

The space frame elements are used to create a long tunnel (5m/1m/1m) that is position at ground level. All of the space frames are closed on the sides by using a perforated black plates (+/-90cm). The perforations (2cm) for constellations which let light through so that traveling through the tunnel creates a light experience that is similar to traveling through space. The side of the space frame elements that faces the ground are covered with sturdy plates (plywood) that are leveled out at each end with a ramp way. In this way, the visitors can lay down on the bike/trolley and push their way through the tunnel to experience the light effects inside. To add to the effect of travel and being in space a series of hand percussion instruments be added to any of the free ends of the space frame elements. Termed, "Wah-bell", the instruments are made out of the same aluminum profiles (25mm/25mm) as the metal frames. The instruments are created by cutting a slot down the length of the profile so that the pitch when hitting the instrument matches the resonance frequency. Visitors simply grab one of the mallets (inserted into the top of the instrument) and bang the instrument ad lib with it. The instrument produces a resonant ringing tone that can be heard in the tunnel as one goes by. Optional: To make the effect of traveling through the tunnel more varied when going forward and then backward through the tunnel, it is proposed to create inserts made from tubes and cut at an angle. Rotating them by by 90 to 180 degrees changes the amount of light that can be seen through the whole and makes this direction dependent. A lens could also be inserted so that the light effect can be processed i.e, amplified, refracted etc. A QR code is mounted on the first Space Frame on each of the sides of it so that it can be accessed by more than one person at a time. It opens up the case study app.
